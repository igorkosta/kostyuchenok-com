# Your AI Strategy Can't Be a Vendor RFP

Every bank says they want to become an AI-driven company. Most of them mean they want to buy AI from someone else and call it a day.

They issue an RFP. They evaluate vendors. They pick a model, negotiate a contract, and check the box. The same way they bought their core banking system twenty years ago. The same way they buy almost everything.

But AI is not a product you procure. It is a capability you build. And a bank that treats it as a vendor purchase will never become an AI-driven company — it will become a dependent one.

Consider what FIS and Fiserv are doing. They are not buying AI tools. They are embedding OpenAI and Anthropic into their platforms. Anthropic's engineers sit inside FIS. OpenAI is co-building agentOS with Fiserv. These are capability partnerships between infrastructure providers and AI companies — and the banks using them are tenants, not owners.

The banks that will actually win with AI are the ones that build their own AI layer. That means investing in data infrastructure, model governance, AI-native engineering talent, and the architectural understanding to know when to build and when to integrate. It means becoming a technology company that happens to have a banking license, not a bank that bought some AI software.

Banks that refuse to become technology companies will be commoditized. Their margins will compress. Their relevance will shrink. And they will wonder why the AI they bought never gave them the advantage they expected.

If your AI strategy starts with an RFP and ends with a signed contract, you are not becoming AI-driven. You are becoming someone else's distribution channel.
