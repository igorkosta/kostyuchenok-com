---
title: "Banks Have to Compete With AI Hyperscalers Now"
date: 2026-06-22
description: Banks have a real competition now
---

Three years ago, the conversation about AI in banking was simple: can we use this for customer support chatbots? Two years ago, it shifted to: can we use this for internal productivity? Last year, it became: can we build our own models or fine-tune existing ones?

This year, the conversation changed again — and most banks haven't noticed.

OpenAI and Anthropic are no longer just model providers. They are becoming infrastructure companies. And they are doing it by partnering with the companies that already run banking — not by replacing them, but by embedding themselves so deeply into the stack that the line between the AI layer and the banking layer disappears.

Two announcements in May 2026 make this concrete.

Fiserv partnered with OpenAI to launch agentOS — an operating system for agentic AI that runs on its core banking and payments platforms. Six banks are already co-developing AI agents with them. Four focus areas: operational agents, core modernization, cybersecurity, and banking-specific AI.

A week earlier, FIS partnered with Anthropic to deploy Claude-powered AI agents across 20,000 financial institutions. The first product is a Financial Crimes AI Agent that compresses AML investigations from hours to minutes. BMO and Amalgamated Bank are deploying it now. Credit decisioning, fraud prevention, and customer onboarding agents are next.

These are not experiments. The two largest banking infrastructure providers in the world have embedded the two leading AI companies directly into their platforms. Anthropic's engineers are sitting inside FIS. OpenAI is co-building with Fiserv. The AI layer is no longer separate from the banking stack — it is becoming the banking stack.

This changes the role of a bank CTO fundamentally. The CTO who treats AI as a vendor procurement decision is already behind. The CTO who understands both banking infrastructure and AI at a deep level — who can evaluate not just the models but how they integrate with the core, how they affect compliance, how they change the architecture — is the one who will determine whether their bank benefits from this shift or gets disintermediated by it.

The window to build that capability internally is closing. The infrastructure providers are moving fast. The question is whether bank leadership is moving fast enough to match them.
